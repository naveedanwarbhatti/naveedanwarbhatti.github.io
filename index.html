Naveed Anwar Bhatti

Welcome to my academic home on the web. I am an Assistant Professor at Lahore University of Management Sciences (LUMS), where I mentor the next generation of systems researchers while directing high-impact initiatives in embedded intelligence.

Prior to LUMS, I was an ERCIM Alain Bensoussan Postdoctoral Fellow with Prof Thiemo Voigt at RISE-SICS in Stockholm. My research investigates the foundations of cyber-physical systems with a particular emphasis on transiently powered platforms, embedded system security, and network forensics.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the Department of Electronics, Information, and Bioengineering (DEIB), Politecnico di Milano, under the guidance of Prof Luca Mottola. Earlier, I contributed to the Systems and Networks Lab at the National University of Computer and Emerging Sciences, working with Associate Professor Affan A. Syed. My academic roots include an MS in Computer Science and a BS in Telecommunications from FAST-NUCES—experiences that seeded a lifelong fascination with crafting dependable, human-centered technology.

Research avenues

From resilient architectures to secure deployments, my research tackles the practical challenges of making ubiquitous computing sustainable.

Cyber-Physical Systems & Intermittent Computing

Designing computing stacks that gracefully tolerate power failures, enabling batteryless devices to deliver dependable sensing and actuation in the wild.

Embedded & Network Security

Developing forensic techniques and protective mechanisms that secure constrained platforms without sacrificing responsiveness or usability.

Sustainable & Responsible IoT

Championing human-centered, environmentally conscious deployments that translate rigorous research into meaningful societal impact.
